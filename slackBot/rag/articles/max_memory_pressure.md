# Relieving System Performance Pressure

## What's happening
Your Mac is under **memory pressure** — this is different from just using a lot of memory.
It means your system is struggling to keep up with demand and may be using your hard drive
as a substitute for RAM (called "swapping"). This significantly slows things down and,
if left unaddressed, can cause apps to crash or your Mac to freeze.

## Here's what to do

1. **Immediately close apps you're not using:**
   - Press **Command + Q** to fully quit an app (not just close its window)
   - Prioritize closing: web browsers with many tabs, video editing software, virtual machines

2. **Check Activity Monitor for the biggest offenders:**
   - Press **Command + Space**, type **Activity Monitor**, press Enter
   - Click the **Memory** tab
   - Look at the **Memory Pressure** graph at the bottom:
     - **Green** = healthy
     - **Yellow** = caution
     - **Red** = critical — act now
   - Quit the top memory-consuming apps

3. **Restart your Mac** — this clears accumulated memory pressure immediately

4. **After restarting**, avoid opening all your apps at once — let your Mac settle for a few minutes first

## Prevent it from coming back
- Keep fewer browser tabs open — consider bookmarking tabs you want to come back to later
- Close apps at the end of the day rather than leaving them running overnight
- Avoid running heavy apps simultaneously (e.g., video calls + screen recording + large spreadsheets)

## If this keeps happening
Recurring memory pressure is a strong signal that your Mac needs more RAM.
Please contact IT and reference: `max_memory_pressure`
