# Speeding Up Your Mac's Startup Time

## What's happening
Your Mac is taking longer than usual to start up. This often means too many apps are
launching automatically at startup, or there are background processes that need attention.

## Here's what to do

1. Click the **Apple menu** () in the top-left corner → choose **System Settings**
2. Click **General** in the sidebar → then click **Login Items**
3. You'll see a list of apps that open automatically when you log in
4. Remove apps you don't need at startup by selecting them and clicking the **−** (minus) button
   - Common ones to remove: Spotify, Teams, Zoom, Slack (you can still open them manually)
   - Keep: security tools, corporate VPN clients, or anything IT has installed
5. **Restart your Mac** to see the improvement

## Additional steps if startup is still slow

- Make sure your Mac's storage isn't nearly full:
  - Click **Apple menu** → **About This Mac** → **More Info** → **Storage**
  - If less than 15% of storage is free, delete old files or move them to an external drive
- Run **Software Update** (Apple menu → System Settings → General → Software Update) —
  pending updates can slow things down

## If this keeps happening
Persistent slow startup times can sometimes point to deeper hardware issues.
If the steps above don't help within a few days, reach out to IT and mention: `avg_boot_time`
