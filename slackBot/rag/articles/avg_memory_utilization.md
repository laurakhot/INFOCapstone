# Reducing Memory Usage on Your Mac

## What's happening
Your Mac is working harder than usual to keep everything running in memory.
When too many apps compete for RAM, your system can slow down, freeze, or crash unexpectedly.

## Here's what to do

1. Press **Command + Space**, type **Activity Monitor**, and press Enter
2. Click the **Memory** tab at the top of the window
3. Look at the **Memory** column — sort it from highest to lowest by clicking the column header
4. Find any app using more than **1.5 GB** that you don't actively need right now, and quit it:
   - Click the app name to select it
   - Click the **X** button in the top-left corner of Activity Monitor
   - Choose **Quit**
5. **Google Chrome** is a common culprit — if it's near the top, try quitting and reopening it
6. If your Mac still feels sluggish after closing apps, a **restart** often helps clear things out

## Quick wins to keep memory healthy
- Avoid leaving dozens of browser tabs open — each tab uses memory
- Quit apps fully (Command + Q) instead of just closing their windows
- Restart your Mac at least once a week if you rarely shut it down

## If this keeps happening
Frequent high memory usage may mean your Mac could benefit from a memory upgrade.
Let IT know and they can assess whether an upgrade is right for your machine.
