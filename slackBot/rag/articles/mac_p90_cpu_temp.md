# High CPU Temperature Detected

Your Mac is running hotter than normal. Sustained high temperatures can shorten component lifespan and cause thermal throttling — where your laptop slows itself down to cool off.

**Steps to reduce CPU temperature:**

1. **Check for runaway processes** — Open Activity Monitor, sort by CPU %, and quit any apps using excessive CPU.
2. **Close unused browser tabs and apps** — Each open tab or app adds heat load.
3. **Check your environment** — Make sure your laptop is on a hard, flat surface. Soft surfaces (pillows, beds) block the vents.
4. **Clean the vents** — Dust buildup restricts airflow. Use compressed air on the vents if accessible.
5. **Restart your laptop** — A fresh restart clears background processes that may be causing heat.

If temperatures remain high after these steps, contact IT and mention `p90_cpu_temp`.
