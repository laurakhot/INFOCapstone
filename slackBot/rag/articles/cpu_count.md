# Your Laptop May Need a Hardware Upgrade

## What's happening
Our system has detected that your laptop's processing capacity is showing signs of strain.

This typically means the hardware inside your Mac, such as the processor or related components, is no longer meeting the demands of your workload. 

Unlike memory pressure or slow startup, this is something software fixes cannot resolve.

## What this means for you
Your laptop will likely continue to work, but you may notice:
- Persistent slowdowns, especially during demanding tasks (video calls, large files, builds)
- Fans running loudly and frequently
- Apps taking much longer to open or respond

## What you should do right now

### Step 1 — Back up your data (most important!)
Make sure everything important is saved before anything happens:

1. Sync your files to **Google Drive** or your company's cloud storage
2. For a complete backup, connect an external drive and use **Time Machine**:
   - Click **Apple menu** () → **System Settings** → **General** → **Time Machine**
   - Click **Add Backup Disk** and follow the steps

### Step 2 — Request an assessment from IT
Contact IT and let them know you've received this alert. Share:
- **Issue type:** Processing capacity (`cpu_count`)
- **Your device name** (Apple menu → About This Mac)

IT will evaluate whether a repair, upgrade, or replacement is the right path forward.

### Step 3 — In the meantime
- Avoid running multiple heavy apps at the same time
- Restart your Mac regularly (every day or two) to keep things running as smoothly as possible
- Keep your software up to date (Apple menu → System Settings → Software Update)

## You're in good hands
IT has helped many people through this process. Reach out to them — they'll make sure
the transition is as smooth as possible and that none of your work is lost.
