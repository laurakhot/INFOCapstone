# Case 4: 😴 Sleep Deprived Laptop

## Here's what to do
1. Click the Apple menu in the top-left corner of your screen
2. Select "Restart" from the dropdown menu
3. Wait for your Mac to completely restart
4. Log in with your credentials when prompted

## If your Mac is unresponsive and won't restart normally:

You can perform a force restart by pressing and holding the power button for 10 seconds until your Mac turns off completely, then wait a few seconds and press the power button once to turn it back on.

## What's happening
* Your laptop has been running for a long time without a restart.
* Over time, this causes memory to build up, background processes to pile on, and performance to gradually degrade — which can eventually lead to slowdowns or unexpected crashes.
* Restarting once a week is a great habit. It clears out memory, applies any pending updates, and keeps your laptop running at its best.
