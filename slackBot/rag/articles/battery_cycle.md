# Your Battery Has Reached End-of-Life

## What's happening
Every rechargeable battery has a limited number of charge cycles. Your Mac's battery
has exceeded its recommended cycle count, which means it can no longer hold a full charge
reliably. This can cause your Mac to:
- Shut down unexpectedly, even when the battery shows charge remaining
- Drain much faster than it used to
- Report inaccurate battery percentages

**This is a hardware issue — software changes won't fix it.**

## What you should do right now

### Step 1 — Back up your data (most important!)
Before anything else, make sure your files are safe:

1. Connect to **Google Drive** or your company's cloud storage and make sure recent files are synced
2. For a full backup, connect an external drive and use **Time Machine**:
   - Click **Apple menu** () → **System Settings** → **General** → **Time Machine**
   - Click **Add Backup Disk** and follow the prompts

### Step 2 — Keep it plugged in
Until you get a replacement, keep your Mac connected to power as much as possible.
Treat the battery as unreliable — don't rely on it for important meetings or deadlines
without power nearby.

### Step 3 — Request a replacement
Contact IT to request a new laptop. When you reach out, share this reference:
- **Issue type:** Battery degradation (`battery_cycle`)
- **Your device name** (you can find it in Apple menu → About This Mac)

IT will guide you through the handover process and make sure your data transfers smoothly.

## You've got time — but don't wait too long
Your laptop won't stop working immediately, but the battery will continue to degrade.
The sooner you start the backup and replacement process, the smoother the transition will be.
